Smart Goals

Many of us are experiencing a drastic change in our
priorities during the pandemic. Whether you are working or
unemployed, take this opportunity to evaluate how you can
use this time wisely. Creating SMART goals is a great way to
develop a plan and stick with it.

Many of us have received a stimulus check recently. This is a great opportunity to create
a SMART goal and decide what you want to do with the money. Some possibilities
include: putting money in savings, paying bills, or splitting it up for necessities.

Eating a healthier diet sounds like a good idea, but what dos it actually mean? Aim for
specific goals such as eliminating soda, or eating five servings of vegetables a day.

Make sure you can measure your goal. Walking four days a week is a goal you can keep
track of.

Remember to choose realistic goals so you don't get discouraged. Losing 10 pounds a
week sounds great, but it is an impossible gol that will likely lead to you giving up on
your efforts.
